Chhatrapati Shivaji Mahara

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630–1680) was the founder of the Maratha Empire in western India and one of the most revered leaders in Indian history. Born in the hill fort of Shivneri, he was raised by his mother, Jijabai, and inspired by her teachings and the tales of valor from the epics. Shivaji was known for his exceptional military strategy, administrative skills, and dedication to Swarajya (self-rule). He established a strong naval force, built a network of forts, and effectively utilized guerrilla warfare to challenge the mighty Mughal Empire and other regional powers. Shivaji was crowned as Chhatrapati (sovereign king) in 1674 at Raigad Fort. His governance promoted justice and welfare, irrespective of caste or religion, earning him the respect and admiration of people across the region. He is celebrated as a symbol of bravery, leadership, and cultural pride in Maharashtra and beyond.
